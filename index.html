A note — please read this.

Coaching and counselling at Clearhead are not a substitute for clinical mental health care. If you are experiencing — or suspect you may be experiencing — a diagnosed mental health condition such as clinical depression, anxiety disorders, OCD, PTSD, psychosis, or any condition requiring medical intervention, please reach out to a licensed psychiatrist or clinical psychologist.

This practice is for people who are fundamentally okay — but want to think better, feel heard, and make decisions they can stand behind. If you're unsure which category you fall into, reach out and I'll be honest with you about whether this is the right fit.

Questions people ask

What's the difference between coaching and counselling?

Coaching focuses on where you're going — decisions, direction, clarity, goals. Counselling focuses on how you're feeling — patterns, emotions, processing what's happened. In practice, most people need both, and this practice holds space for both. You don't need to decide which one you need before you come. We'll find what's actually useful.

Is this therapy?

No. This is coaching and counselling — not clinical therapy. It's not suited for diagnosed mental health conditions. Think of it as rigorous, confidential support — with someone trained to help you think and feel heard.

Do I need to have a problem to come?

No. Most people who come aren't in crisis. They just have a full head and nowhere to put it. A decision pending. A feeling they can't name. You don't need to be struggling to deserve space to think.

What if I don't know what to talk about?

That's fine. We start wherever you are. Sometimes the most valuable sessions begin with "I don't know where to start" — because that's actually a very accurate description of what's going on.

Is everything confidential?

Fully. ICF ethics and professional counselling codes bind this practice absolutely. Nothing you say leaves this space.

Online or in-person — does it matter?

Both work well. In-person sessions are in Lokhandwala, Andheri West. Online sessions run across India. The quality of listening is the same either way.

How many sessions will I need?

Honestly — sometimes one is enough. Most people find 3–4 meaningful. We don't lock you into a plan. You come when it's useful.

Can coaching help me navigate the AI wave?

Yes — and this comes up a lot. The AI conversation most people need isn't about tools or prompts. It's about identity: who you are when your skills shift, what you actually want before the market decides for you, and how to move with intention instead of just reacting. If you're carrying AI anxiety, this is a useful space for it.

Do you work with international clients?

Yes. Online sessions run across time zones. The work is the same whether you're in Mumbai, Bangalore, London, or San Francisco.
