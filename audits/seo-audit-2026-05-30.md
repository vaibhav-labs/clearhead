# SEO Audit — Clearhead — 2026-05-30

## Summary
The site's SEO foundation is solid: every indexable page has a unique title under 60 chars, a description in the 120–160 range, a self-referential canonical, complete OG/Twitter cards, and well-structured JSON-LD. Live homepage matches local byte-for-byte. The most material gap is that `runway.html` is the only calculator missing `SoftwareApplication` schema — the other three tools have it. After that, the issues are sitemap `lastmod` drift, stale `dateModified` values in `Article` schema, an off-spec OG image aspect ratio on the AI-loneliness post, and uneven internal linking from the homepage to the three newest tools and one blog post.

## Critical (0)
None.

## High (1)
- **`runway.html` is missing `SoftwareApplication` JSON-LD** — `runway.html:38-69` only contains `BreadcrumbList`. The other three calculators (`bandwidth.html`, `career-friction.html`, `ai-relevance.html`) each include a `SoftwareApplication` block alongside their breadcrumb. CLAUDE.md also states each tool should carry it. Add a `SoftwareApplication` schema block describing the Abundance Runway Calculator (name, applicationCategory: "FinanceApplication", operatingSystem: "Any", offers price 0, url, description) to bring it to parity.

## Medium (5)
- **Sitemap `lastmod` values are stale for 7 of 12 URLs** — `sitemap.xml:5,11,17,23,29,35,41`. Files modified on 2026-05-29 or 2026-05-30 still carry `<lastmod>` of 2026-05-15/16/27. Google uses `lastmod` as a recrawl signal; with the site already battling slow indexation (per CLAUDE.md), stale values undercut a free freshness ping. Refresh all `<lastmod>` to match actual file modification dates, then resubmit the sitemap in GSC.
- **`Article` `dateModified` never updates** — 4 of 5 posts declare `"datePublished":"2025-04-01","dateModified":"2025-04-01"` (`post-ai.html`, `post-unheard.html`, `post-lonely.html`, `post-conversation.html`), and `post-ai-loneliness.html` shows both dates as `2026-05-15` despite a `2026-05-30` file change. If the posts have only had cosmetic edits (meta, schema, links), this is acceptable; if any body copy has changed, bump `dateModified` on the affected posts. Setting `dateModified` honestly is one of the simplest freshness signals for an article.
- **`post-ai-loneliness.html` OG image is portrait** — `post-ai-loneliness.html:18-21`. The image is an external Unsplash URL declared as `og:image:width=1200`, `og:image:height=1800` (3:2 portrait). Twitter `summary_large_image` and most social previews crop or letterbox anything not roughly 1.91:1 landscape, so the share preview will look off. Either swap to a 1200×630 landscape crop (Unsplash supports `&h=630&fit=crop`) or revert to `VJ.jpg` like the other posts. Also: depending on an Unsplash hotlink puts the share preview at the mercy of a third party — a locally hosted image is more durable.
- **Three calculator pages are orphan from the homepage** — `bandwidth.html`, `career-friction.html`, and `ai-relevance.html` are only linked from `tools.html`. The homepage already links directly to `runway.html`. Either link the three new calculators directly somewhere on the homepage (sessions section, footer, or a small "Free tools" strip near the runway mention), or at minimum confirm the `tools.html` link in the primary nav is enough discovery for crawlers. Right now they are two clicks from the front door, which combined with the site's low crawl budget will delay indexation.
- **`post-conversation.html` is not linked from the homepage** — every other blog post (`post-ai-loneliness`, `post-ai`, `post-unheard`, `post-lonely`) is. Inside the body of `index.html` the post appears in neither the hero, the cards, nor the contact block. Add one inbound link from the homepage to level the field, or accept it and remove from sitemap priority.

## Low (5)
- **Duplicate image files in repo** — `1.jpg` / `2.jpg` / `3.jpg` are byte-identical in size to `coaching-grad.jpg` / `coaching-mid.jpg` / `coaching-early.jpg` (271645 / 268267 / 254856). Likely leftover originals. Not an SEO issue, but they're deployed publicly and bloat the build. Verify with `md5sum`, delete if confirmed.
- **`robots` meta inconsistency** — tools declare `index, follow, max-image-preview:large`; blog posts only declare `index, follow`. Add `, max-image-preview:large` to the 5 `post-*.html` files so blog images can show full-size in Discover and image-rich SERPs.
- **`keywords` meta on `index.html:9`** — Google has ignored this since ~2009. Harmless but signals "I'm doing SEO from a 2010 checklist" if anyone audits the source. Safe to delete.
- **`blog.html` canonical points to `.html` extension** — `blog.html:10` uses `https://clearhead.in/blog.html`. Netlify Pretty URLs serve both `/blog` and `/blog.html` as 200, so this isn't broken, but external sharers and internal nav use the `.html` form already, so this is consistent. No action needed unless a future migration switches to clean URLs everywhere; flagging only for completeness.
- **Cross-post linking is uneven** — `post-ai-loneliness.html` (the newest and most search-targeted post) has no inbound links from any peer post; meanwhile `post-lonely.html` has 6 inbound links. Add a "Related" or in-body link from the older posts to `post-ai-loneliness` so PageRank flows toward the freshest, most query-rich page.

## What's already strong

- Every indexable page has a self-referential canonical, OG image with `width/height/alt`, Twitter `summary_large_image`, and `geo.region` for local SEO.
- Homepage `FAQPage` JSON-LD contains 9 questions that match the 9 visible `<summary>` headings exactly — no schema/content drift.
- Homepage carries the full `Person` + `ProfessionalService` + `LocalBusiness` triplet with `geo` coordinates and `hasMap` — strong local-business signal.
- All 5 blog posts have `Article` + `BreadcrumbList` + `FAQPage` schema (BreadcrumbList correctly 3-level Home → Blog → Post).
- All images carry meaningful `alt` text; no `<img>` tags missing alt.
- No external `<a target="_blank">` links missing `rel` attributes (spot-checked all indexable HTML).
- Heading hierarchy clean across blog posts: exactly one `<h1>`, then `<h2>`/`<h3>` with no skipped levels.
- `quiz.html` and `thank-you.html` both correctly noindexed; `quiz.html` also disallowed in `robots.txt`.
- All 12 sitemap URLs return HTTP 200 on live; live homepage `<head>` byte-identical to local.
- Largest image is 271 KB — well under the 500 KB flag threshold; no page-speed risk from imagery.
