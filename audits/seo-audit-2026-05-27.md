# SEO Audit — Clearhead — 2026-05-27

## Summary

The site is in solid SEO shape: structured data is thorough and correct, images are well-optimised, all external links are properly secured, and the technical foundation (canonical tags, robots.txt, sitemap, heading hierarchy) is clean. The two items that most need attention are both title-tag truncations — the homepage title at 105 characters is being cut nearly in half in search results, weakening the very keywords this site needs to rank for. Most meta descriptions are also significantly over the recommended length and will be rewritten by Google rather than displayed as authored. Everything else is low-severity.

---

## High (2)

- **Homepage title severely truncated** — `index.html:7` — At 105 characters, Google shows roughly "Clearhead Mumbai | Vaibhav Jain — Coaching & Counselling for AI An…" and drops "Mid-Career Identity & Loneliness" entirely. Shorten to ≤60 chars, e.g. `Clearhead Mumbai | Vaibhav Jain — ICF PCC Coach & Counsellor`.

- **post-ai.html title truncated** — `post-ai.html:7` — At 87 characters, "You Don't Have to Lose Yourself With It." is cut. Shorten to ≤60 chars, e.g. `Your Job Title Is Changing. Don't Lose Yourself. | Clearhead`.

---

## Medium (3)

- **Meta descriptions overlength across all pages** — `index.html:3` (263 chars), `post-ai-loneliness.html:4` (229 chars), `post-lonely.html:4` (200 chars), `post-unheard.html:4` (198 chars), `post-ai.html:4` (194 chars), `post-conversation.html:4` (194 chars) — Google truncates at ~160 characters and may substitute its own snippet instead. The descriptions are well-written; they just need to be trimmed to 120–160 characters each. The homepage description (currently 263 chars) is the most urgent — it includes credentials and location that are worth preserving.

- **Three more titles slightly over 60 chars** — `blog.html:7` (69 chars), `post-ai-loneliness.html:7` (68 chars), `post-lonely.html:7` (66 chars) — These are at the edge and may or may not truncate depending on pixel width. Worth tightening, e.g. removing ` | Clearhead Mumbai` from post titles and instead appending ` | Clearhead`.

- **Missing `og:image:width` and `og:image:height` on all pages** — `index.html`, all post pages, `blog.html` — Without these tags, Facebook and LinkedIn must download the image to determine its dimensions before generating a rich preview, which slows link previews and can cause them to fall back to a smaller card. Add `<meta property="og:image:width" content="1200" />` and `<meta property="og:image:height" content="849" />` to every page that uses `VJ.jpg` as the OG image. (For `post-ai-loneliness.html`, which uses an Unsplash image, check dimensions and add accordingly.)

---

## Low (4)

- **`quiz.html` missing canonical tag** — `quiz.html` — No `<link rel="canonical">` is present (though `og:url` is). Since `/quiz.html` is already disallowed in `robots.txt`, search engines won't index it, so the risk is minimal — but adding the canonical is a one-line fix that closes the gap cleanly.

- **Potential `.html`-vs-clean-URL duplicate content** — Netlify's Pretty URLs feature means `/blog` and `/blog.html` likely both serve the same content. The canonical tags all point to `.html` URLs (e.g. `https://clearhead.in/blog.html`), which handles consolidation for Google — but it's worth verifying in Netlify's dashboard that the non-.html URLs either redirect to the canonical or that Google Search Console shows no duplicate coverage issues. If `/blog` is being indexed alongside `/blog.html`, add a 301 redirect rule in `netlify.toml`.

- **Missing `og:image:alt` across all pages** — All pages use `og:image` but none include `og:image:alt`. This tag improves accessibility for screen-reader users encountering social previews and is recommended by the Open Graph spec. Add `<meta property="og:image:alt" content="Vaibhav Jain — ICF PCC certified coach, Mumbai" />` to all pages using `VJ.jpg`.

- **`privacy-policy.html` and `terms.html` in sitemap** — `sitemap.xml:19-26` — These utility pages don't contribute to search visibility and add noise to crawl budget. Removing them from the sitemap is a minor cleanliness improvement with no downside.

- **Structured data is thorough**: FAQPage on homepage matches every visible FAQ item exactly; all five blog posts carry Article schema + BreadcrumbList (3-level: Home → Blog → Post); homepage carries LocalBusiness with `geo`, `hasMap`, `telephone`, `openingHours`, `address`.
- **Single H1 per page, no skipped heading levels** — clean hierarchy across all pages.
- **Canonical tags are present on all indexable pages** — correct self-referencing canonicals throughout.
- **`thank-you.html` correctly set to `noindex, nofollow`** and quiz.html is correctly disallowed in `robots.txt`.
- **Sitemap is current** (last-modified dates in May 2026) and references the correct `https://` URLs.
- **All images have meaningful, descriptive alt text** — not generic "image" placeholders.
- **OG and Twitter Card meta is complete** on all pages (title, description, image, card type, locale).
- **Security headers configured** in `netlify.toml` (X-Content-Type-Options, X-Frame-Options, Referrer-Policy).
- **No render-blocking scripts** — Razorpay and Cal.com scripts load from footer, after page content.
