# Content Quality & E-E-A-T Audit — clearhead.in

**Scope:** Blog corpus (26 `post-*.html` files in the repo; 23 live, 3 pending deploy — a known, already-flagged
timing issue, not re-reported here) plus homepage author/credential signals that individual posts inherit or link to.
**Method note:** this session had no live-fetch/browser tool available, so the assessment was run directly against
the local source files that Netlify auto-deploys verbatim (per `KNOWLEDGE_MAP.md` §10) — content for already-live
pages should match production exactly. No claim is made about external citation URLs actually resolving; that would
need a follow-up pass with fetch access.

## Summary

The blog corpus is, on the whole, unusually strong for a small coaching site: 21 of 26 posts are grounded in a
specific, named, linked peer-reviewed study or industry survey (author, journal/publisher, year, DOI or URL),
carry matching `Article`/`FAQPage`/`BreadcrumbList` JSON-LD, and use an answer-first FAQ structure that is well
suited to AI-citation. Word counts are comfortably above the thin-content floor everywhere checked (roughly
700–2,000+ words per post). The two genuine gaps are (1) five early posts that predate the research-driven
editorial format and lean on unattributed claims like "study after study" instead of a named source, and (2)
individual blog post pages carry no visible, clickable author-bio/credentials block — the ICF PCC credential and
"3,000+ conversations" trust signal only live on the homepage `#about` anchor, which nothing on a post page links
to. Both are fixable without touching the site's voice.

## Findings

| Issue | Severity | Evidence | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Individual blog posts have no visible, linked author-bio/credentials block | Medium | Every post's byline is plain, unlinked text: `<p class="post-meta">On AI & identity · 7 min read · Clearhead, Mumbai</p>` (e.g. `post-ai.html:103`) and the footer `<div class="post-footer"><p>From Clearhead · ICF PCC certified coach & counsellor, Lokhandwala, Mumbai</p>` (e.g. `post-monday-dread.html:315`) is also plain text, not a link. Post-page nav only offers "What this is" (`index.html#what`), not an About/author link. The only place credentials, the "3,000+ conversations" stat, and the `vj9.org/human` writing link appear is the homepage `#about` section — unreachable from a post in one click. JSON-LD `author.sameAs` does carry the link, but that's invisible to a human reader and to any AI system reading the extracted page text rather than structured data. | Add a small, visible author byline card (photo optional, credential line, 1-sentence bio, link to `index.html#about` or a dedicated `/about.html`) near the top or bottom of every post. This is the single highest-leverage E-E-A-T fix given how much AI-citation and trust depends on a reader/crawler being able to verify who wrote the piece without leaving the page. |
| 5 of 26 posts lack a specific, named, cited source — rely on unattributed generalizations | Medium-High (given the business's AI-citation growth strategy) | Confirmed via grep: `post-conversation.html`, `post-unheard.html`, `post-ai.html`, `post-lonely.html`, and `post-new-city-loneliness.html` are the only posts with **no** `"citation"` JSON-LD block. In the body text: `post-lonely.html` — "Study after study, survey after survey, the finding is the same" (no source named); `post-new-city-loneliness.html` FAQ — "Adult relocation loneliness is widely documented" (no source, no link). Contrast with the 21 other posts, which name the study, author, journal, and year (e.g. `post-monday-dread.html`, `post-quiet-cracking.html`, `post-mattering-at-work.html`). | Bring these 5 posts up to the same evidentiary standard as the rest of the corpus — add at least one named, linked study per post (even a single citation would close the gap). Since AI-citation is explicitly the growth lever here, unattributed claims are the pieces least likely to ever get quoted by an answer engine, and the most exposed to a "thin/generic" read under the Sept 2025 QRG. |
| Some citations point to a general hub page rather than the specific source | Low | `post-ai.html` "Further reading" links to `mckinsey.com/capabilities/mckinsey-digital/our-insights` — a rotating insights hub, not the specific report being referenced (contrast with the DOI-level precision used in `post-monday-dread.html`, `post-quiet-cracking.html`, `post-mattering-at-work.html`). | Where possible, link to the exact article/report rather than a topic hub, so the citation is verifiable and stable over time (hub URLs change content silently). |
| Minor image metadata mismatch on one post | Low | `post-new-city-loneliness.html`: `og:image` meta tag (line 17) points to `https://clearhead.in/og-new-city-loneliness.jpg`, but the `Article` JSON-LD `"image"` field (line 48) points to a different Unsplash URL. Social previews and structured-data image extraction could disagree on which image represents the piece. | Align the two to the same canonical hero image. |
| Five closely-related "AI & work identity" posts target overlapping queries | Low | `post-ai.html`, `post-ai-job-fear.html`, `post-ai-replacement-dysfunction.html`, `post-ai-work-family-exhaustion.html`, and `post-technostress.html` are all distinctly sourced (good) but all answer variations of "is AI going to take my job and what do I do about the anxiety" — a plausible keyword-cannibalization risk once these are indexed. | Not urgent pre-indexing, but once in GSC, watch for cannibalization between these five and consider a pillar/hub post that explicitly differentiates and links to all five by their distinct angle (identity, clinical-name fear, family spillover, tool-fatigue, career-displacement). |

## What's already strong

- **Research grounding is genuinely rare-good for this category of site.** 21 of 26 posts cite a real, named, dated study with author and publication (e.g. Chandola et al. 2025 in *Journal of Affective Disorders*, a 2026 *Journal of Management* review, a 2025 TalentLMS survey, a 2025/2026 *Clinical Psychology Review* meta-analysis on mattering), each backed by a matching `citation` JSON-LD block and a visible "References" list with working-looking outbound links.
- **Honest evidence-caveating.** Several posts explicitly flag study limitations rather than overstating findings — e.g. `post-monday-dread.html` ("This is a study of older adults in England... shows an association, not a proven chain of cause and effect") and `post-mattering-at-work.html` ("the York workplace study is a small pilot of 60 adults... its exact numbers should be read as an early signal"). This is exactly the kind of trustworthiness signal Quality Raters and AI systems reward, and it's a genuine differentiator versus typical AI-generated wellness content.
- **Answer-first, extractable structure.** Every post ends with a "Common questions about X" FAQ section (4 Q&As) that mirrors the `FAQPage` schema verbatim, gives quotable, self-contained answers with real numbers (23% cortisol increase, 19% heart-attack rise, 30% feeling invisible, etc.), and is well-positioned for AI answer-engine extraction.
- **No thin content found.** Every post checked (including the shortest, "4 min read" pieces) comfortably clears a ~300-word informational-content floor; the research-grounded posts run 1,500–2,000+ words of substantive body copy.
- **Real experience signals.** First-person practitioner framing ("When I work with professionals navigating the AI shift...", "I've sat with a lot of people who thought they were confused...") paired with specific, varied testimonial roles (Senior PM, Engineering Director, VP Strategy) reads as credible rather than templated.
- **Solid trust infrastructure site-wide:** physical address, phone/WhatsApp, email, ICF PCC credential referenced consistently, privacy policy and terms linked from every footer, and a clear non-therapy disclaimer on the homepage.
- **The 3 not-yet-live posts** (`post-manager-burnout.html`, `post-self-compassion-work-stress.html`, `post-mattering-at-work.html`) are at or above the quality bar of the live corpus — all three carry proper citations, and `post-mattering-at-work.html` in particular is one of the better-caveated pieces on the whole site. No content-quality work is needed before they go live.
