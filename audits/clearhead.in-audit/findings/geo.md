# GEO / AI-Search Readiness Audit — clearhead.in

**Audited:** 2026-07-14
**Scope:** Live site (clearhead.in, /blog, post pages) + local repo (`~/Documents/Zen`) — `robots.txt`, `llms.txt`, `sitemap.xml`, `index.html`, and 25 `post-*.html` files.
**Context:** Per the client's `clearhead-visibility-os` playbook, AI-citation (Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT, Perplexity, Bing Copilot) is prioritized over classic rank tracking. This audit is scored accordingly.

---

## Summary

Clearhead's content engineering is genuinely ahead of most small-business sites for AI-citation readiness: every post published from 2026-07-08 onward opens with a named, dated study, carries `Article` + `FAQPage` + `BreadcrumbList` JSON-LD with a `citation` array pointing to real DOIs, closes with a "References" section, and is delivered as static server-rendered HTML with zero client-side rendering dependency — the single best thing a site can do for AI crawler accessibility. `robots.txt` is fully open (no bot-specific blocks at all, so GPTBot/ClaudeBot/PerplexityBot/OAI-SearchBot/Google-Extended are all implicitly allowed). `llms.txt` exists and is well-formed.

However, the site has grown fast (6 posts → 25 posts, plus 6 interactive tools/quiz pages) and the GEO scaffolding has not kept pace in three specific ways: (1) **`llms.txt` is stale** — it lists only 7 of 25 published articles and omits the entire interactive-tools section (`runway`, `bandwidth`, `career-friction`, `ai-relevance`, `readiness`, `still-on-your-list`), which are high-value, highly citable, self-contained answer engines; (2) **the "named study" pattern the client is known for does not hold across the whole catalog** — the original 4-6 posts (`post-ai`, `post-unheard`, `post-lonely`, `post-conversation`) carry no citations, no references, and no stats, unlike every post from `post-ai-loneliness.html` onward; (3) **`dateModified` in Article schema is stale/inconsistent with `sitemap.xml`** for those same older posts, which risks AI crawlers deprioritizing genuinely-current content as outdated. Entity/authority signals (Person schema, ICF credential markup) are solid but narrow — the only `sameAs` identity is a personal site (`vj9.org/human`); there is no LinkedIn, Wikipedia, YouTube channel, or Reddit presence wired in anywhere, which matters given YouTube/Reddit/Wikipedia are the strongest documented correlates of AI citation.

## Findings

| `llms.txt` lists only 7 of 25 live articles; entirely omits the tools hub and 6 tool/utility pages | **High** | `/llms.txt` "## Articles" has 7 entries (`post-ai`, `post-ai-loneliness`, `post-unheard`, `post-lonely`, `post-conversation`, `post-manager-burnout`, `post-mattering-at-work`). `sitemap.xml` has 25 `post-*.html` URLs plus `tools.html`, `runway.html`, `bandwidth.html`, `career-friction.html`, `ai-relevance.html`, `readiness.html`, `still-on-your-list.html` — none of the tools are in `llms.txt` at all, and 18 posts (incl. `post-perfectionism.html`, published 2026-07-12, 2 days before this audit) are missing. | Regenerate `llms.txt` to enumerate all indexed posts and all 6 tools (each tool is a self-contained, citable "answer engine" — arguably the highest-value inclusion). Add this to the existing `clearhead-site-monitor` automation so it can't drift again. |
| "Named study" citation pattern does not hold for the original posts | **High** | `post-ai.html`, `post-unheard.html`, `post-lonely.html`, `post-conversation.html` (all `datePublished: 2025-04-01`) have **no** `citation` array in JSON-LD, no References section, and no in-body named study/stat — confirmed by direct read of all four. Contrast with `post-manager-burnout.html`, `post-perfectionism.html`, `post-ai-loneliness.html`, `post-mattering-at-work.html`, etc., which all open with a dated, named study (Gallup 2025, Frontiers in Psychiatry 2025, Li et al. 2026) and carry full `citation` + References. | Retrofit these 4 legacy posts with a named study opener, `citation` JSON-LD, and a References block to match the now-established house style — these are exactly the pages an AI engine is least likely to cite as-is today. |
| `dateModified` in Article schema is stale and inconsistent with `sitemap.xml` `lastmod` for legacy posts | **Medium** | `post-ai.html`, `post-unheard.html`, `post-lonely.html`, `post-conversation.html` all show `"dateModified": "2026-05-30"` in JSON-LD, but `sitemap.xml` lists `lastmod: 2026-07-08` for the same URLs — a 39-day gap with no corresponding record of what changed. Newer posts (`post-manager-burnout.html`, `post-perfectionism.html`) have matching sitemap/schema dates. | Either genuinely re-touch these 4 posts (recommended, given finding above) and update both dates together, or stop bulk-touching `sitemap.xml` `lastmod` on unchanged files — the mismatch itself is a minor trust signal risk for freshness-sensitive AI crawlers. |
| No high-authority entity signals beyond a personal site | **Medium** | Person schema `sameAs` across every page = `["https://www.vj9.org/human", ".../google maps link"]` only. GSC status per `KNOWLEDGE_MAP.md` is still "Discovered — not indexed," consistent with a young, low-authority domain. | Add a LinkedIn profile URL to `sameAs` at minimum (near-zero effort, real signal). Medium-term: publish 1-2 of the existing long-form posts as LinkedIn articles/short video, and seed a Reddit presence (e.g., answering in r/india, r/ExperiencedDevs-style career-anxiety threads) — both are cited in GEO literature as stronger citation predictors than backlinks. |
| Body content headings (H2s) are declarative, not question-based, except the closing FAQ section | **Low** | E.g. `post-perfectionism.html` H2s: "There are two kinds of perfectionism, and only one of them hurts," "It is rising, and it is not a personal weakness" — strong narrative voice, but only the final "Common questions about X" section uses question phrasing. This is already compensated by the `FAQPage` schema, but direct in-body question headings (H2/H3) are an independent, complementary citability signal AI answer engines look for outside of schema parsing. | Low priority given FAQ schema already covers this — but consider adding 1-2 question-phrased subheadings per post (e.g. reframe "What actually helps it move" style H2s as literal questions) for incremental lift without disrupting the brand voice. |
| `robots.txt` has no explicit AI-crawler allow rules | **Info / Low** | `robots.txt` is `User-agent: * / Allow: /` with 4 disallows (`/thank-you.html`, `/quiz.html`, `/api/`, `/.netlify/`) — no bot-specific stanzas for GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot, OAI-SearchBot, Google-Extended, or CCBot. | Not a defect — the wildcard `Allow: /` already permits every AI crawler by default, including the ones the client wants (GPTBot, ClaudeBot, PerplexityBot, OAI-SearchBot). Optional: add explicit named `User-agent` stanzas for those four for clarity/auditability, and consider whether to explicitly disallow `CCBot`/`anthropic-ai` (training-only crawlers) if the client wants citation-visibility without also feeding training corpora — currently they're allowed too since the wildcard covers everyone. |
| No RSL 1.0 licensing signal | **Info / Low** | No `license` metadata, no RSL file, no `<meta name="license">` found alongside `llms.txt`. | Optional given RSL adoption is still nascent; low priority relative to the `llms.txt` and citation gaps above. |

Newer posts' body paragraphs (e.g. the Müller/Kubátová paragraph in `post-manager-burnout.html`, ~140 words) land closer to the sweet spot. | Not urgent — short, precise answers are still citable and match the brand's spare tone. If optimizing further, expand 1-2 sentences of context/mechanism into the shorter FAQ answers to nudge them toward ~150 words without padding.

## What's already strong

- **Zero rendering barrier for AI crawlers.** The whole site is static HTML/CSS/vanilla JS with no build step or client-side rendering — there is no SSR-vs-CSR gap at all. This is the technical-accessibility dimension essentially maxed out.
- **`robots.txt` blocks nothing an AI crawler needs.** No GPTBot/ClaudeBot/PerplexityBot/Google-Extended disallow anywhere; only low-value utility pages (`quiz.html`, `thank-you.html`, `/api/`) are excluded.
- **`llms.txt` exists and is well-formed** (correct Markdown structure, concise summary, categorized links) — it's stale, not broken, which is a much easier fix.
- **Rigorous, citation-grade structured data on every post published since 2026-07-08**: `Article` (with `citation[]` pointing to real DOI/journal URLs), `BreadcrumbList`, `FAQPage` with self-contained `acceptedAnswer` text, plus an in-body "References" section with full academic citations. This is materially better schema/citation hygiene than the vast majority of small-business or coaching sites.
- **Consistent "named study" opening pattern holds for 19 of 25 posts** — Gallup 2025, Frontiers in Psychiatry 2025, a 2025 Journal of Occupational and Organizational Psychology meta-analysis, a 2026 loneliness/chatbot study (Li et al.), etc. — giving AI engines a concrete, attributable, quotable claim near the top of the page.
- **Strong, consistent E-E-A-T markup**: `Person` schema for Vaibhav Jain with `hasCredential` (ICF PCC via International Coaching Federation), `LocalBusiness`/`ProfessionalService` with geo-coordinates, address, phone, and `FAQPage` on the homepage — entity disambiguation for "Clearhead" and "Vaibhav Jain" is clear and consistent across every page examined.
- **Internal linking to interactive tools from within blog posts** (`bandwidth.html`, `career-friction.html`) turns narrative content into an on-ramp to genuinely useful, self-contained "answer engines" — a good multi-modal/utility signal, once those tools are also surfaced in `llms.txt`.

## AI Search Readiness Score: 72 / 100

**Rationale (weighted by the framework's 5 dimensions, but read against this client's AI-citation-first strategy):**

| Dimension | Weight | Score | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| Citability | 25% | 78/100 | Excellent on 19/25 posts (named study, direct claims, references); the 4 legacy posts with no stats/citations drag the average down materially since they're still indexed and linked from the homepage/blog index. |
| Structural Readability | 20% | 80/100 | Consistent H1/H2 structure, FAQ blocks, short paragraphs, clean HTML — docked slightly for declarative (non-question) H2s outside the FAQ block. |
| Multi-Modal Content | 15% | 55/100 | Good: interactive tools (calculators) are genuinely differentiated multi-modal content. Weak: no owned video/YouTube presence, images are all generic stock (Unsplash) with no original diagrams/data visualizations of the cited stats. |
| Authority & Brand Signals | 20% | 60/100 | Strong on-page E-E-A-T (Person + credential schema, NAP consistency) but very thin off-page entity graph — no LinkedIn, Wikipedia, YouTube, or Reddit `sameAs`; GSC still shows pages as not-yet-indexed, meaning even Google's classic index (a precursor to AI Overview eligibility) hasn't fully caught up yet. |
| Technical Accessibility | 20% | 95/100 | Static HTML, no CSR, open robots.txt, present llms.txt, clean sitemap — essentially nothing blocking a crawler from reading and extracting content. |

**Overall: 72/100 — "Strong foundation, uneven execution across content age."** The newest ~19 posts, if they were the whole site, would score in the mid-80s. The score is held back not by anything broken, but by drift: `llms.txt` not tracking the now-25-post catalog, 4 early posts not yet upgraded to the citation pattern the rest of the site has adopted, and an entity graph that's well-structured but has almost no external reinforcement (LinkedIn/Wikipedia/YouTube/Reddit) — which the data shows matters more to AI-citation likelihood than the backlink/domain-authority metrics this client has already deprioritized. The two "High" severity fixes above (refresh `llms.txt`, backfill citations on the 4 legacy posts) are both low-effort, no-redesign changes that would likely move this into the low-80s.
