# Clearhead — 360° Design Audit & Executable Redesign Report

**Date:** 2026-07-07 · **Auditor:** Claude (full-render audit of live site at 1440px and 390px, every fold, plus code inspection)
**Grounding:** benchmarked against Melody Wilding, Reboot.io, Everbe Therapy, The Thought Co. (Mumbai), Amaha, and 2025–26 coaching/therapy design-trend research. Audience held constant: Indian mid-career professionals (28–50) with AI anxiety, identity questions, loneliness — booking paid 1:1 work.

**Verdict in one line:** The bones are excellent — voice, comic strip, pricing transparency, dark bookends — but the homepage is ~2× too long, the hero hides the one thing this category must show (the coach's face), one section is visually broken on desktop, and dead third-party JS ships on every homepage load. Cut a third, fix four bugs, move the face and one testimonial up, and this site outperforms every direct competitor.

---

## P0 — Bugs to fix immediately (all verified in live render)

**1. Disclaimer card is broken on desktop.** `styles.css:2265-2270` sets `.disclaimer-inner { display:grid; grid-template-columns: 44px 1fr }` with an `::before` icon. Grid auto-placement puts the first `<p>` into the 44px icon column — the whole paragraph renders one word per line down a 44px strip. Live at 1440px right now.
*Fix:* wrap the h2+p's in a `<div>` in `index.html` (disclaimer section), or make the `::before` `position:absolute` and drop the grid.

**2. Dead Cal.com embed on the homepage.** `index.html:641-649` initializes three Cal namespaces; zero elements on the page use `data-cal-*` (verified by grep — no matches sitewide). Every visitor downloads cal.com's embed JS for nothing; all booking CTAs are plain links.
*Fix:* delete the whole embed `<script>` block from index.html.

**3. `<button>` vs `<a>` primary buttons don't match.** `.btn-primary` (styles.css:149) never resets the UA border, so real `<button>`s ("Send a quiet hello", "Pay for Starter/…", quiz "Next", runway "Start") render with a dark ring + native look that the `<a>` buttons ("Book the free 30-minute conversation", "Choose Starter") don't have. Visible on pricing, contact, quiz, runway.
*Fix:* add `border:0; appearance:none; cursor:pointer; font-family:inherit;` to `.btn-primary`.

**4. Contact-form consent checkbox is misaligned** (mobile especially): bare white UA checkbox floats above the label line on the dark band.
*Fix:* `.consent-check { display:flex; gap:.6rem; align-items:flex-start; }` + `accent-color: var(--ember)`.

## P1 — The five changes that will actually move bookings

**1. Put Vaibhav's face in the hero.** Every benchmark solo practice (Everbe, Reboot, Wilding) leads with the real human; generic stock is the #1 "dated" marker in this category. The current hero image is an anonymous dark silhouette from Unsplash — a stranger, facing away, in the one slot that sells trust. `VJ.jpg` already exists and is warm and professional but is buried in section 8.
*Execute:* replace `index.html:226` hero `<img>` (Unsplash) with a well-cropped VJ photo (shoulders-up, looking toward the copy). Keep it visible on mobile too (`styles.css:120` currently `display:none`s the hero visual — show a smaller version instead of hiding the only face).

**2. Cut the homepage from 11 sections to 7.** It's 10,931px tall on desktop, 13,525px on mobile — roughly double the benchmark. The funnel section ("Begin with a free 30-minute conversation") is 6 sections deep. Recommended order, with what gets cut:

| # | Keep | Action |
|---|------|--------|
| 1 | Hero (with real photo) | keep |
| 2 | "You might recognise this" | keep — best empathic-mirror copy on the site |
| 3 | Free 30-min conversation CTA | **move up** from position 6 + place one testimonial card beside it |
| 4 | Comic strip ("A first session") | keep — most distinctive asset on the site |
| 5 | About + credibility | keep; **merge the AI-band's cred line into it** |
| 6 | Testimonials (remaining two) + disclaimer card | merge into one band |
| 7 | FAQ → Contact (dark) | keep; **cut the map section** — fold "Open in Google Maps →" link into the contact side column |
| — | AI & Identity band | **cut as a section** — keep the excellent "talking to ChatGPT isn't the same" aside as a small card inside §2, link to the AI post. The 8 floating tag-pills scan as decoration |
| — | Runway widget band | **cut from home** — one line + link under the free-conversation section ("Free 2-minute tools →"). Tools have their own hub + nav item |
| — | Blog preview | **6 cards → 3**, single row |
| — | Map iframe | **cut** (heavy third-party load that renders as a giant white box; the address + link do the job) |

**3. One number, once.** "3,000+ conversations" appears in the hero eyebrow, the hero stats, and twice in About. Repetition reads as insecurity. Keep it in the stats row and once in About; slim the eyebrow to `ICF PCC · Coach & Counsellor · Mumbai`.

**4. Make WhatsApp a first-class action.** India-specific research is unambiguous ("people don't fill forms, they WhatsApp"; The Thought Co. leads with it). Currently it's one link at the bottom of the contact card.
*Execute:* add WhatsApp as the secondary action next to the hero CTA or as a small fixed pill on mobile, using a pre-filled deep link: `https://wa.me/919028902948?text=Hi%20Vaibhav%2C%20I%27d%20like%20to%20talk.` Also add it to the footer.

**5. Collapse the pricing page's duplicate purchase flow.** "Choose Starter/Going Deeper/Long Game" buttons only anchor down to a second set of three near-identical cards with "Pay for X" buttons — six buttons for three products, and the choice context (price, inclusions) is gone by the time you can pay.
*Execute:* put the `pay-btn` directly on each package card; delete the "Pay for your package" card grid; keep the Razorpay terms line + status element under the cards. Add one reassurance line: *"After payment, you'll pick your first slot on the calendar right away."*

## P2 — Craft & polish (do after P1)

**Imagery system.** The site currently speaks two visual languages: moody Unsplash photography (hero, 3 cards, 36 images on the blog index) and the warm hand-drawn comic/flat-illustration style. The comic is the brand. Extend it: replace the three "You might recognise this" stock photos with three small drawn vignettes in the comic style (the rejected peep set proves illustration was already the instinct). Bonus: kills three more Unsplash hotlink dependencies. The current three photos are also all dark-lonely-silhouette clichés — heavier than the "calm, mature, no hype" brand voice.

**Blog index diet.** 9,453px tall, 36 hotlinked Unsplash images, internal posts and external vj9.org links visually identical. Actions: thumbnails only for internal posts (text-only rows for external links), or self-host + compress thumbnails; cap each theme at 3 visible with a "more" expander. The blank-thumbnail effect users on slow connections see mid-scroll is lazy-load latency from hotlinking — self-hosting fixes it.

**Adjacent same-tint bands.** About (`band-alt`) is immediately followed by Testimonials (`band-alt`) — they fuse into one undifferentiated grey slab. The P1 reorder fixes this; otherwise alternate.

**Warm the neutrals slightly.** Industry palette trend is warm/earthy (terracotta, sand, sage) for safety-and-trust; Clearhead's cool mist + indigo reads slightly fintech. Don't repaint v2 — just (a) use the ember accent more in illustration details, (b) consider nudging `--bg` a touch warmer (e.g. `#f4f5f2` family), (c) keep navy bookends.

**Micro-copy tightening (word-level pass):**

| Where | Now | Change to | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| Nav CTA | "Say hello" | "Book a free call" | The single nav CTA slot should carry the booking action, not a greeting |
| Comic section h2 | "A first session, drawn out." | "A first session — in four panels." | "Drawn out" reads as *tedious* to skimmers before the pun lands |
| Contact submit | "Send a quiet hello" | "Send — I reply within a day" | Converts the reassurance from the sub-line into the button itself |
| Hero eyebrow | 4 facts crammed | "ICF PCC · Coach & Counsellor · Mumbai" | See P1-3 |
| Badges "· New" (AI band, runway) | "New" | drop | "New" badges age silently and are already stale |
| Stats row | "PCC / ICF certified" as a number-slot | swap to "15+ yrs" or move PCC to eyebrow | A word in the count-up number row breaks the pattern (`anim.js` count-up skips it) |
| Pay section | — | add "After payment, you'll pick your first slot right away." | Removes the last unknown before paying |

**Add a 3-step "how it works" strip** (benchmark: The Thought Co.'s "it's smaller than you think"): `Free 30-min call → Choose a package (or don't) → One session every 2–3 weeks`. Three lines under the free-conversation section. This is the highest-leverage anxiety-reducer the site is missing; the comic tells the *feeling*, this tells the *process*.

**Trust chips.** Six self-declared claim-chips is one too many rows (6 stacked rows on mobile). Keep the strongest four ("Google, Meta & Apple professionals", "Fortune 500 leadership", "Founders & senior engineers", "International clients, all time zones"). "IIT & top-college alumni" + "Global consulting & Big-4" overlap with the others.

**FAQ additions.** Benchmarks pre-answer: *"What happens in the first session?"* and *"How do payments and rescheduling work?"* Both answers already exist as copy elsewhere — surface them as two more `<details>`.

**Accessibility quick wins.** (a) Quiz disabled "Next" salmon state — add `opacity` + `cursor:not-allowed` so it reads *disabled*, not *broken*. (b) The hamburger `aria-expanded` toggle is correct as-is (verified); leave it. (c) Keep the count-up respecting `prefers-reduced-motion` (already good).

## What NOT to change

The brand voice (best-in-category — "Nothing leaves this room", the sceptical-engineer testimonial, "Names withheld — that's the rule here, not a choice"). The comic strip. Per-session price framing with package totals in schema. The free-30-min offer itself (perfectly matches the Indian intro-call norm). The disclaimer's honesty (fix its layout, keep every word). Bricolage Grotesque + Figtree pairing. The dark hero/contact bookends. Netlify Forms honeypot + consent line. The tools as lead magnets.
